# Peer Review Assignment

## Learning Objective

By completing this assignment, you should be able to evaluate your teammates fairly, reflect on how each member contributed to the group, and provide useful feedback about teamwork during group assignments and the final term project.

## Activity Type

Individual assignment.

Each student must complete this peer review independently.

## Instructions

Complete one peer review for every other student in your project group.

Do not review yourself.

Your review should be fair, specific, and based on your actual experience working with the person. This is not only a popularity rating. You should consider how the person communicated, accepted responsibility, worked with others, and helped the group complete assignments and the final term project.

Your peer review will be used as part of the teamwork or contribution score. The instructor may also use project evidence, submitted work, Git evidence, in-class observations, and team reports when deciding the final score.

## Required Review Categories

For each teammate, give a score from 5 to 0 for each category.

| Score | Meaning |
|---:|---|
| 5 | Excellent; consistently strong contribution or behavior |
| 4 | Good; usually strong, with only minor issues |
| 3 | Acceptable; contributed enough, but not especially strong |
| 2 | Weak; inconsistent, passive, or often needed reminders |
| 1 | Very weak; rarely helped or caused repeated problems |
| 0 | No meaningful contribution, absent, or seriously harmful to the team |

Use the following categories:

| Category | What to Consider |
|---|---|
| Communication | Did the person respond, explain progress, ask questions, and keep the team informed? |
| Responsibility | Did the person complete assigned tasks, meet deadlines, and take ownership of work? |
| Friendliness / Temperament | Was the person respectful, cooperative, calm, and reasonable to work with? |
| Usefulness During Group Assignments | Did the person help the team during in-class or homework group tasks, not only the final project? |
| Usefulness During Final Term Project | Did the person make a meaningful contribution to the final project product, code, report, demo, or coordination? |

Each teammate review has a maximum of 25 points.

## Written Feedback

After rating each teammate, write a short comment.

Your comment should mention:

- what the teammate did well
- what the teammate could improve
- any important contribution issue the instructor should know

Keep your writing professional. Do not insult the person. Focus on behavior, contribution, and evidence.

## Submission

Submit the completed peer review.

Your submission file should follow:

- Convert the google doc into PDF
- rename your submission file to "[YourFirstName]_[FirstThreeLettersOfLastName]_peer_review.pdf
- Make sure to include your name and student ID, group name

## Confidentiality

Your peer review should be honest and professional.

The instructor will use the reviews for grading, but the exact content of your review will not be shared directly with other students as a named comment.

## Important Notes

If you do not submit the peer review, you may lose marks for this category.

If there is a serious team problem, explain it clearly and briefly in the written feedback section.
